the first prisoner, Ceres, to be bound to a cross, to be dismembered from the bottom up, with the coup de grâce, afterwards his dead body to be dragged to the outer place of execution, there to be laid upon a wheel, and to remain thus as prey to the air and the birds of heaven; further the second prisoner, April van Ceijlon, to be exposed with a rope around the neck under the gallows, then to be tied to a stake, severely scourged on the bare back with rods, thereafter to be riveted in chains and to labour in them for a period of five successive years without wages on the public works on Robben Island, and after the termination of this time, to be returned to his master
